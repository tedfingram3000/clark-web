KEY MATCHUPS & WATCHLIST

Jordan Walker vs deGrom. Walker carries the loudest RISP profile in the Cardinals lineup -- .299 AVG / .982 OPS in 78 PA -- and bats cleanup against a pitcher who has allowed 13 HR in 59.2 IP. deGrom's 35.2% GB rate keeps the ball in the air, exactly the contact type Walker punishes. If Wetherholt or Herrera reach in front of him, the cleanup spot is the swing-the-game at-bat.

Ezequiel Duran vs McGreevy. Duran is at .419 AVG / 1.007 OPS in 47 PA with runners in scoring position -- the loudest situational bat in the Texas lineup. McGreevy's 53.1% GB rate and 6.3% BB% form the counter: keep him off base, keep the ball on the ground when he is up. Any inning Duran reaches the batter's box with a runner in scoring position is the inning where this game can flip.

Alec Burleson vs deGrom. Burleson's .328 AVG / .851 OPS RISP profile (75 PA) hits left-handed in the three-hole, immediately ahead of Walker. The L-R sequence in the heart of the order is the leverage spot -- if deGrom navigates Wetherholt and Herrera early, Burleson is the pivot from contact to power.

Josh Jung vs McGreevy. Jung sits at .341 AVG / 1.001 OPS RISP in 50 PA -- nearly a mirror of Duran's profile from the right side. McGreevy will pitch carefully when Jung and Duran are back-to-back, and the at-bat to mark is the one where McGreevy goes to 3-2 with the ground-ball pattern still working.

X-factor: deGrom's third pass through the order. deGrom averages roughly 5.4 IP per start (59.2 IP across 11 GS), which puts the Cardinals in the heart of the third pass through the order at the 4th-5th turnover. If the offense forces him to that pass, the bullpen has to cover the back end -- and that handoff is the seam Cardinals lineups have worked all year.
