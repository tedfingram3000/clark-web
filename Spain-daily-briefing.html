The shape of the day

You land jet-lagged with hours to kill before the 3 PM room. The art of day one is staying upright until a normal bedtime — light activity, daylight, no long horizontal naps.

Options & ins/outs

  • Bags before check-in: the Hyatt will hold luggage if the room isn't ready. Drop bags, then walk — don't sit.
  • Bus tour (in your notes): the hop-on Madrid City Tour has a stop near Gran Vía / Plaza España. A loop is the lowest-effort way to stay awake and oriented while horizontal-adjacent.
  • Gentle alternative: Retiro Park is a 20-min walk — flat, green, easy to bail from. The Book Fair (Feria del Libro) runs along the Paseo de Coches if it's still on.
  • La Carmencita is on C/ Libertad in Chueca, ~15 min walk or short taxi from the hotel. It's one of Madrid's oldest taverns (1854) — your "second oldest restaurant" plan.

Concerns

Don't nap past ~3 PM and not more than 45 min. The 8 PM reservation is your anchor to reset to Spanish time.
Cash: hit an ATM in T4 arrivals or near the hotel — take €100 notes or smaller (Fresco's advice). Tell your bank you're in Spain so the first charge doesn't freeze the card.

Madrid at full speed

Madrid Open day · Botín 8 PM
DinnerSobrino de Botín · 8 PMWorld's oldest restaurant (1725) · roast suckling pig
DaytimeWide openYour one fully free Madrid day

The shape of the day

Your only unscheduled full day in Madrid. Worth a loose plan so it doesn't evaporate. 8 PM at Botín is early by local standards (locals eat 9:30–10:30) — which is good, it leaves the evening open after.

Options — pick a lane

  • Art morning: the Golden Triangle — Prado, Reina Sofía (Guernica), Thyssen — all within 10 min of each other. Pick one; don't try all three. Prado is free 6–8 PM but that clashes with Botín.
  • PHotoESPAÑA 2026: the photography festival is opening now across Reina Sofía, CaixaForum, Círculo de Bellas Artes. Low-cost, atmospheric, very Madrid-in-June.
  • Royal core: Royal Palace + Almudena Cathedral + Plaza Mayor, then drift to La Latina for tapas. This also pre-walks tomorrow's Changing of the Guard ground.
  • Rooftop hour: Círculo de Bellas Artes azotea or the Riu rooftop at Plaza España for the late golden light before dinner.

Concerns & ins/outs

Botín books out. Confirm the reservation is held and arrive on time — they seat the famous cochinillo (suckling pig) and cordero (lamb) from the original wood oven. It's the signature; order it.
Pace it: tomorrow is your biggest Madrid day (guard + bullfight). Keep tonight from running too late.
Heat: June Madrid afternoons run hot and dry. Do museums/indoor 1–5 PM, save streets for morning and evening.

Guard, bullfight & the market

Madrid Guard noon · Las Ventas 7 PM
NoonSolemn Changing of the GuardRoyal Palace · 1st Wed of month · free
7 PMBullfight · Las VentasSan Isidro · 6 Lagunajanda bulls · ~2h
AlsoMercado de San MiguelTapas market near Plaza Mayor

The shape of the day

Two ticketed/timed anchors at opposite ends of the day with a long free afternoon between. Morning = palace. Afternoon = rest/market. Evening = Las Ventas.

Changing of the Guard — ins/outs

  • The solemn ceremony (full band, cavalry) happens the first Wednesday monthly — June 3 qualifies. Far bigger than the weekly change.
  • Arrive by 11:30 for a spot along the Palacio Real / Plaza de la Armería railings. It runs ~30 min.
  • It's outdoors and sunny — hat, water.

Bullfight — seating you've researched

  • Best first-timer seat: Tendido Preferente (Shade) €96.80–€238.70 — close, padded, always shaded.
  • Value shade: sections 1, 2, 9, 10 from €33 (higher rows).
  • Budget: Sol sections 5–7 from €20.50 — full sun, but bearable at a 7 PM June start.
  • Buy via the official Plaza 1 / Janto site only. Avoid lasventas.com (expired/parked).
If tickets aren't yet bought — this is the one item that can sell out. Lock seats before the day. It's a San Isidro corrida, the prestige season.

Afternoon & after

  • Mercado de San Miguel (your note): touristy but fun for grazing — go off-peak (3–5 PM) to dodge crowds, or after the bullfight if you want late bites.
  • Las Ventas is on Line 2 (metro Ventas), ~15 min from center. Taxi back after dark is easy.
  • Optional late: Noches del Botánico concert series may have an opening night near this date.
Pack tonight. Tomorrow is a travel day to Sarria and the Camino begins. Your big bag gets transferred hotel-to-hotel on the walk — but you need walking kit accessible.

The shape of the day

Transition day. Morning in Madrid, ~4h train northwest into Galicia, evening orientation. Tonight you get your Orientation Packets, first Pilgrim's Passport stamp, and a Galician dinner (included).

Logistics — ins/outs

  • Station is Chamartín, not Atocha. Allow time — Chamartín is north of center, ~15–20 min taxi from Gran Vía. Be there 30+ min early; gates close 2 min before departure.
  • This is the Alvia (loc RXF4WD) — not the AVLO return train. Tickets are the June-4 Omio "Mobile" set.
  • Seats 5A (Robert) / 5B (Margaret), Car 8. No catering on board ("Sin Restauración") — grab lunch at Chamartín first.
  • 17:32 arrival gives a comfortable buffer before the 7:30 PM meet at Hotel Mar de Plata.

Camino bag strategy (matters from here on)

One bag per person (max 20 kg / 44 lb) is transferred hotel-to-hotel each day by Fresco. The transport company is strict on weight. Carry a daypack with water, rain shell, snacks, blister kit, passport/phone — everything you need on the trail, since you won't see the big bag until the next hotel.
Your trail shoes are the whole trip. Broken in, lightweight, waterproof. Galicia can rain any day in June.

The arrival

6 days, 115 km — you reach the cathedral. This is the emotional summit of the trip.

What to do, in order

  • Pilgrims' Office (Rúa Carretas 33): final stamp + request your Compostela certificate. Lines build midday — go on arrival or early.
  • Pilgrims' Mass at the cathedral, noon (also evening masses). The botafumeiro (giant incense censer) swings on some days — not guaranteed.
  • Wander the medieval old town — Praza do Obradoiro, the cathedral facade, the buzz of arriving pilgrims.
Two passport stamps a day minimum over the last 100 km are what unlock the Compostela. If you've been collecting, you're set.
Hotel Compostela for the night of the 10th is your tour's final-night room (booked via Fresco). The post-tour nights (11–13) are separate reservations — see next days.

Finisterre — the End of the World

Coast excursion Optional · must pre-book
Free dayNo fixed plansBest slot for the Finisterre trip
SleepHotel Compostela

The big option: Cape Finisterre

Your one fully open day in Santiago. The classic add-on is the Costa da Morte / Finisterre coast — the "End of the World" lighthouse the Romans believed marked the edge of the earth.

Excursion choices (book via Fresco / Camino Angel by email)

  • Standard group tour — €49 pp, ~9h, full day. Best value. Includes Pontemaceira, Muxía sanctuary, Cape Finisterre, Ézaro waterfall, Carnota's long hórreo, Muros. Escort + English audioguides. Pickup at Capilla del Pilar, Alameda Park.
  • Private coastal — €369 (1–4 pax), 6–7h. Muros, Carnota, Ézaro, Finisterre. Door-to-door, taxi-style, no guide.
  • Private interior — €189 (1–4 pax), ~4h. Straight to the cape and back. Quickest.
  • Add Muxía (+€49/group) to either private option — 12th-c. clifftop sanctuary.
Pre-book required. Email your Fresco service manager (Camino Angel) ahead — these don't run on walk-up. For two people, the €49 pp group tour is far cheaper than private.
Lunch in Finisterre town isn't included on any option. Recs: O Centolo (Paseo Ribeira 3) or La Fontana — both seafood.
